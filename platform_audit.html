      07
      Strategic Positioning

      What This Means for Matt

      The technical findings directly determine Matt's negotiating position. The distinction matters enormously.

      This is a completion job, not a stabilization job

      Stabilization means existing features work and you're polishing and hardening. That's a contractor or advisor role — valuable, but not foundational.

      Completion means core features are incomplete — you're building 40–60% of the platform's working functionality from scratch. That's a co-founder role, not a contractor role.

      Based on the audit: the Financial module (Plaid, reconciliation, reporting), Project Hub (contractor syncing, milestone tracking), Markus AI (data connectivity), and multi-tenancy architecture are all unbuilt or incomplete. These workstreams are not polish — they are the product. If you build them, you built the platform.

      What equity should anchor on

      The $72K–$96K value of the full build (at $150–200/hr market rate) is the floor for any equity-in-lieu-of-cash conversation. If Mitch and Joe are asking you to forego that cash in exchange for equity, the equity stake must represent that value plus a premium for the risk of not getting paid now.

      Typical anchor for a technical co-founder joining a pre-revenue startup with an existing early scaffold: 15–30% equity, 4-year vest with 1-year cliff. The range depends on how much of the core technical work is yours vs. theirs, and whether you're also contributing strategic direction, commercialization thinking, and investor relationships.

      Mitch's email language — "technical partnership," "commercialization strategy," "scalable SaaS business," "potentially valuable acquisition target" — signals they're already framing this as co-founder territory. Don't let them walk that back when it comes to formalizing the structure.

      The one risk to name explicitly

      The social dynamic (Dan → neighbor → friendship) creates pressure to move fast and trust informally. Don't. Good friends make the worst informal business partners. The structure protects the friendship, it doesn't threaten it.

      Before any significant Phase 1 work begins: get a signed consulting agreement that defines scope, rate, deliverables, and that Matt retains no IP claims from Phase 1 work (or the opposite — negotiate which way this cuts). Before Phase 2 begins: get the partnership agreement, cap table, and IP ownership documented.

      The ChatGPT notes from your prior research said it best: "The real risk is becoming strategically indispensable before compensation and equity structures are formalized." That risk is real and imminent here.
